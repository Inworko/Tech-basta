e-Basta Kit

Our initiative e-Basta, is a comprehensive educational kit designed for learners. It encompasses high-end e-resources, robust assessment tools, and advanced personalized learning algorithms and impact measurement tools to enhance learning outcomes. Furthermore, it aims to enhance learners' skills, preparing them effectively for both personal and professional endeavors.

e-Basta Implementation

The project targets learners and schools of underprivileged segment. The training is provided to the educators to the purpose and usage of this kit. Teachers use this kit in the classroom and formative assessment is done for evaluation of the learning outcomes suing the assessment tools which are part of this kit. Basis the analytics received remedial class are run to improvise the outcome at higher level.

Advanced tools are provided during the learning process in the classroom to make the understanding of the concepts effective. The 3D resources, simulation, self-learning activities learning games are some of the few resources which are used to create an interactive learning environment in the classroom.

How Our Team Works

Our dedicated team of educators, highly trained in technology, subject matter expertise, and modern pedagogical methods, visits communities to set up interactive digital environments. These environments are equipped with cutting-edge digital tools that create an engaging and participative learning atmosphere, distinctly different from traditional classrooms. Within these digital spaces, our educators work closely with groups of underprivileged students, focusing on enhancing their conceptual knowledge through interactive and immersive experiences. This innovative approach not only makes learning more accessible but also significantly improves the students' understanding and retention of core concepts.

e-Basta Process

The e-Basta program begins with a comprehensive initial assessment to evaluate students' proficiency in core subjects such as Mathematics, English, Hindi, and Environmental Science (EVS). In addition to academic skills, we also observe and measure students' behavioral skills and language proficiency. This preliminary evaluation provides a clear understanding of each student's baseline knowledge and capabilities, allowing us to tailor our educational resources and interventions to effectively address their specific needs and foster holistic development.

Post Assessment Structure

After preliminary assessment, the students were shown interactive multimedia modules from the e-Basta kit on the particular topic and made to repeat along. They were given learning modules and assessment tools to evaluate the change after using the kit. After the session, students were given another assessment to check how much they had understood from the training session. Initially, the before and after assessments were paper worksheets, but as the students began to grow more accustomed to technology, assessments began to be conducted on tablets. The use of interactive educational technology enabled the kids to become better learners as it held their attention and forced them to think in different ways to solve the problem.

Post-Assessment Structure

Following the initial assessment, students were introduced to interactive multimedia modules from the e-Basta kit on specific topics, which they engaged with actively. They were then provided with learning modules and assessment tools to measure their progress. At the end of the session, another assessment was administered to gauge their understanding of the material covered. Initially, these assessments were conducted using paper worksheets. However, as students became more familiar with technology, assessments transitioned to being conducted on tablets and desktops/Laptops. The implementation of interactive educational technology not only captured students' attention but also encouraged them to think critically and solve problems in diverse ways, thereby enhancing their overall learning experience.

Outcomes

The program's impact is evaluated across various dimensions, including students' grasping ability, comprehension of different subjects, maintenance of sanitation, behavioral skills, and language development. To measure these outcomes, we employ a range of assessment tools and devices that analyze students' learning progress across different subjects and topics. By collecting data before and after implementing the program, we can accurately assess the improvement in students' understanding and mastery of specific concepts. This comprehensive analysis enables us to pinpoint areas of progress and identify where further support may be needed, ensuring a holistic approach to educational enhancement and student development.
